# GlowGuide Constitution

## 1. User Safety First

GlowGuide must provide educational personal care guidance without making medical claims. The platform must avoid diagnosing skin conditions, promising results, or replacing professional advice. Any concern that may require clinical care must be accompanied by a recommendation to consult a dermatologist or qualified healthcare professional.

## 2. Beginner-Friendly Language

All content must be understandable for users who are new to skincare, bodycare, and hygiene routines. Product terms, routine steps, ingredients, and recommendations must be explained in simple language without unnecessary jargon.

## 3. Inclusive Personal Care

GlowGuide must support common skin types and concerns including oily, dry, combination, normal, and sensitive skin. Content should be respectful, non-judgmental, and inclusive of different budgets, routines, and experience levels.

## 4. Structured, Database-Driven Content

Product categories, routine steps, concerns, skin types, ingredients, and recommendations must be stored as structured data in the database. The frontend should display recommendations by filtering and matching this structured content instead of hardcoding all results directly into UI components.

## 5. Clear Separation of Education and Recommendation

The platform must distinguish between general educational information and personalized recommendations. Recommendation results should explain why a product category or routine step is suggested for a selected skin type or concern.

## 6. Privacy by Design

The initial version must not collect sensitive personal data. If user accounts or saved routines are added later, the system must collect only necessary information and clearly explain how user data is used.

## 7. Hackathon Delivery Standard

The first version should prioritize a complete, demo-ready experience over excessive feature breadth. The minimum product must include navigation, educational sections, product library, routine builder, backend API, and database-backed recommendation data.

## 8. Open Contribution With Review

GlowGuide should encourage public contribution while protecting users from inaccurate or unsafe content. Contributor submissions must be reviewed before they affect live educational content or recommendation results. The project should provide contribution guidelines, content quality rules, and a transparent status for submitted content.

## 9. Source-Aware Content

Contributor-submitted information should include a short explanation and, where possible, a source or reason for the suggestion. Medical, clinical, or result-guaranteeing claims must not be accepted without review by qualified maintainers or converted into safer educational wording.
